  • Proportional (P): Your immediate reaction to how far you are from the target. Like steering a car back to the center of the lane based on how far off you are.
  • Integral (I): Considers the history of errors. If you’ve been off course for a while, it builds up to nudge you back. Like gradually correcting a persistent drift.
  • Derivative (D): Predicts future errors by observing how quickly the error is changing. It slows you down if you’re about to overcorrect. Like easing off the wheel to avoid overshooting.
  • Reference (Setpoint/Target): The goal or desired state, such as the temperature you want a thermostat to maintain.
  • External Force (Disturbance): Unexpected influences that push you off course, like a gust of wind or an open window, which the controller must compensate for.
